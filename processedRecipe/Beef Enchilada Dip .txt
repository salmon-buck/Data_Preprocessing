 1 : Preheat oven to 400 degrees F. Lightly oil two 10-ounce ramekins or coat with nonstick spray. 2 : Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium high heat. Add ground beef and cook until browned, about 3-5 minutes, making sure to crumble the beef as it cooks
 drain excess fat. Stir in enchilada sauce, corn, chiles, 1/2 cup Monterey Jack cheese and 1/2 cup cheddar cheese
 season with salt and pepper, to taste. 3 : Divide the filling evenly into the ramekins and top with remaining cheeses. 4 : Place into oven and bake until bubbly around the edges, about 5 minutes. Let cool 5 minutes before serving.
